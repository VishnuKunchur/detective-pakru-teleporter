The Adventures of Detective Pakru

Detective Pakru and the Teleportation Device


The August twilight was still warm when Detective Pakru yawned and finally mustered the will to switch on her table-fan.


It had been several weeks since she had last solved a case and business was dull, much like the grey Fogust mornings during that time of the year. She opened the Doordash app on her phone, and considered ordering in a boba. Then she paused to check her bank balance on the Chase app, and decided against ordering in boba. Her stomach rumbled, and she thought it might be a good idea to watch some Kerala street food vlogs. Then she started to dream of Keezhattingal. And then, soothed by the gentle winds from her trusty table-fan, she nodded off on her couch.


She woke up, startled at the tone of a flurry of incoming messages on her phone. Wait - they did not sound like her regular text messages. Could it be - could Acchu really be sending messages on പറയു (parayu, which is Malayalam for "speak")? Acchu and Pakru's friendship went back all the way to Keezhattingal, long before Acchu became fascinated with computers and code. When they were in middle school Acchu built പറയു, a private messaging mobile app that Pakru and Acchu used to exchange secret messages sometimes. Today, Pakru's പറയു alerts seemed to be pouring in at a relentless pace. Acchu, or Dr Aswati Unnikrishnan, was among the most prolific computer scientist - social activists of her generation and worked at ICHEP, a physics research institute that did some pretty cutting edge research. Beyond that, Pakru did not know much about ICHEP. She secretly felt like she knew even less about Acchu now than she knew about ICHEP, given that it had been about 5 years since they had last spoken.
